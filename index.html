      Methodology

      Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI)

      The map displays the Transparency International CPI 2024 score for each country. CPI aggregates 13 independent expert and business surveys (e.g. World Bank CPIA, World Justice Project Rule of Law Index, Bertelsmann Foundation, IMD World Competitiveness Yearbook, PRS International Country Risk Guide) to estimate perceived public sector corruption on a normalized 0–100 scale. A country is included only if at least three eligible source data points are available.

      • 0 = highly corrupt; 100 = very clean.
      • Scores are perception-based — they measure expert/business assessment of corruption, not directly observed corrupt acts.
      • Year-over-year changes within ±2 points are not statistically significant per TI's own guidance.

      Choropleth construction

      • Geometry: Natural Earth simplified polygons, served as countries-110m.json from the world-atlas package via jsDelivr.
      • Projection: Natural Earth I (d3.geoNaturalEarth1), tuned to viewport.
      • Join key: ISO 3166-1 numeric (TopoJSON feature.id) → ISO 3166-1 alpha-3 → CPI score.
      • Color scale: piecewise-linear from deep red (0) → orange (25) → yellow (50) → light green (75) → deep green (100). Clamped at the endpoints.
      • No-data: territories without CPI 2024 coverage (e.g. small dependencies, disputed regions) render as neutral grey.

      Derived statistics

      • Rank is computed at load time from the loaded CPI dictionary using dense ranking (ties share a rank).
      • Percentile = round(((N − rank + 1) / N) × 100), where N is the number of countries with CPI 2024 data in this build.
      • Tier bins: ≥80 Very clean • 65–79 Clean • 50–64 Mixed • 30–49 Corrupt • <30 Highly corrupt.

      Financial crime feed

      • The live panel renders an illustrative stream of enforcement actions, sanctions, AML penalties, indictments, asset seizures, and active investigations.
      • Seed events have synthetic timestamps offset from page load so the feed appears recent on every visit; the stream emits a new event every 5–13 seconds from a curated pool.
      • Selecting a country on the map scopes the feed to that jurisdiction; chip filters narrow by event type.
      • For production use, the seed should be replaced with live ingest. Suggested feeds: OFAC SDN delta, UK OFSI consolidated list, EU sanctions map, FATF black/grey lists, FinCEN advisories, SEC / DOJ press releases, Europol press, ICIJ leaks, and national FIU bulletins.

      Limitations

      • CPI measures perception, not incidence. It does not capture private-sector corruption, money laundering volumes, or illicit financial flows directly.
      • Geometry is simplified at 1:110m; small island states may be invisible at default zoom.
      • Score values shipped in data.js are static; refresh annually when TI publishes the next CPI release.
      • The crime feed is a demonstration scaffold — do not treat events as breaking news.
